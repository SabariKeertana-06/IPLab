Christ The Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer, a colossal iconic statue of Jesus Christ, stands atop the Corcovado mountain
overlooking Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Completed in 1931, this magnificent sculpture is a symbol of
Brazilian Christianity and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Designed by Heitor da Silva Costa and
sculpted by Paul Landowski, the statue rises 30 meters (98 feet) and spans 28 meters (92 feet) from
fingertip to fingertip. Its outstretched arms convey a sense of divine embrace, and the panoramic
views from the summit offer a breathtaking panorama of the city and its natural surroundings. Christ
the Redeemer is a testament to the intersection of faith, art, and engineering, drawing millions of visitors
who are captivated by its spiritual significance and commanding presence against the backdrop of Rio's
picturesque landscape.
